                        ------------------------
                        Gallery Management Guide
                        ------------------------
This guide will help you manage the photos on your website. You only need to work with two locations: the img/gallery/ folder and the gallery.json file.

Important Rules
  Filenames: The name you type in the gallery.json file must match the actual file name in your img/gallery/ folder exactly (including capital letters and the .jpg or .png ending).
  ---
  The Comma Rule: Every entry in the list must end with a comma (,) except for the very last item. If you break this rule, the gallery may stop appearing.
-------------------------
Step-by-Step Instructions
-------------------------
  1. Adding a New Picture
    Place your image file into the img/gallery/ folder.
    ---
    Open gallery.json in your text editor.
    ---
    Find the "images": [ section.
    ---
    Paste your new entry (see the template below).
    ---
    If you are adding this to the middle of the list, ensure the line has a comma at the end.

  2. Managing Captions
    To Add/Update: Find the entry for your photo in gallery.json and type your text inside the quotes for "caption".
    ---
    To Remove a Caption: Just leave the quotes empty, like this: "caption": ""

  3. Removing a Picture
    Delete the file: Delete the image from the img/gallery/ folder.
    ---
    Delete the entry: Delete the entire line for that image in gallery.json. If you deleted the last item in the list, make sure to remove the comma from the line before it.


  4. Copy/Paste Templates
    When adding a new photo, copy one of these blocks and paste it into your gallery.json file inside the square brackets [ ].
    ---
    Template A: Adding a photo WITH a caption
      { "filename": "your-photo-name.jpg", "caption": "A beautiful description here" },
    ---
    Template B: Adding a photo WITHOUT a caption
      { "filename": "your-photo-name.jpg", "caption": "" },

Pro-Tip for Reordering
  You can change the order of the photos on the website simply by highlighting a full line in gallery.json (including the comma), cutting it, and pasting it in a different position within the list.
