Our classes

At Saint Joseph's Atelier

The program is a holistic art curriculum where students encounter many media throughout the semester.

Students explore a wide variety of artistic techniques while learning from master artists and the beauty of God's creation. Each semester combines art history, drawing, painting, printmaking, mixed media, and creative exploration. Rather than following templates, students are guided through meaningful projects that encourage original thinking, careful observation, and artistic growth in a peaceful, faith-centered environment.

Drawing & Observation

Students learn to slow down and truly see. Through nature studies, still lifes, and observational drawing exercises, young artists develop confidence in sketching, proportion, line, and composition.

Painting & Color Theory

Explore watercolor, tempera, and acrylic painting while learning about color mixing, values, shades, tints, light, and movement inspired by artists such as Van Gogh and Georgia O'Keeffe.

Printmaking & Design

Students discover the principles of design through projects such as linoleum block printing, pattern creation, composition studies, and graphic art techniques.

Sacred Art Projects

Faith and creativity come together through projects inspired by the Sacred Heart, Christ the King, Saint Joseph, icons, and other beautiful traditions of Catholic art.

Mixed Media Exploration

Students experiment with chalk pastels, oil pastels, collage, ink, watercolor resist, and a variety of materials that encourage creativity and artistic discovery.

Art Lab & Creative Choice

Inspired by Montessori principles, Art Lab time allows students to explore materials independently, revisit favorite techniques, and create original works from their own imagination.
