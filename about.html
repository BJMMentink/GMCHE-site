About the Instructor

Your Art Instructor

Since I was a small child I have relished opportunities to draw and create. I earned a Bachelor of Fine Arts in 1986. After University, I earned a living freelancing design and illustration jobs. Later I discerned religious life with a contemplative community of sisters in France. Returning home to Texas, I decided to enroll in a graduate program to obtain my certification in Education. Over the years I taught in Catholic, Private and Public Schools in regular education classrooms for students in grades K-7. There I always sought opportunities to integrate hands-on art projects in every subject – whether it was teaching language arts, science or history. I was hired for my dream job in 2007: I became an Art Specialist for an elementary school for the Northside School District in San Antonio, Texas. I love art education because I love sharing what I have learned through my training and experience with various media.
In 2011, I married my beloved husband and moved to the beautiful state of Wisconsin. Four years later we adopted a sibling group from the Philippines and we homeschooled our children. Homeschooling provided many opportunities to work with our own three children and their peers and friends. I taught art to children in a Catholic homeschool co-op, to a group of teenage young ladies in a “Little Flowers” (Little Women’s) program, and then later as a homeschool art instructor.

Hello! I am Donna Zoller-Warner

My Background

Over the years I have taught in a variety of settings including traditional classrooms, Catholic schools, private schools, and homeschool environments. My work has always focused on integrating art into learning across subjects such as language arts, science, and history.

Our Teaching Philosophy

Saint Joseph’s Atelier is a place of learning and working for Catholic Children of God who wish to explore and look deeply, to discover and work mindfully - praising and delighting in God our Father through creativity.

We engage with elements of art and principles of design and seek classic artists as models and teachers who have embraced beauty, truth, and goodness through a variety of media.

We have fun discovering gifts that God has planted in each one of us, knowing that as we learn and express ourselves through visual arts, we are praising and delighting in our Father as He delights in us.

Teaching Approach

Each session begins with prayer so our focus is on glorifying God. Each art session should be a space for learning from a place of rest.

I focus on creating a peaceful, structured, and respectful environment where students feel safe to explore creativity while developing skills and confidence.
